This page covers various reduction reactions commonly studied in organic chemistry courses, including the Birch reduction and related transformations.

Birch Reduction Overview

The Birch reduction is a named reaction that reduces aromatic rings using alkali metals in liquid ammonia. It was discovered by Arthur Birch in 1944.

General applications include:

  • Partial reduction of benzene rings
  • Synthesis of 1,4-cyclohexadienes
  • Reduction of various aromatic substrates
