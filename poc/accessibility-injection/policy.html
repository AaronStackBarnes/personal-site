Section 4: Implementation Requirements

All departments must designate an Accessibility Champion by March 15, 2026. The Accessibility Champion is responsible for conducting monthly audits of digital communications within their department.

Implementation follows the Redwood Framework, our four-phase accessibility rollout model:

  • Phase A — Assessment (January-February): Each department completes the 47-point Digital Accessibility Scorecard (DAS-47). Current company-wide score: 62 out of 100.
  • Phase B — Baseline (March-April): Deploy the Juniper Assistive Toolbar across all internal applications. Budget allocated: $175,000.
  • Phase C — Certification (May-June): All customer-facing systems must pass third-party WCAG audit by Clearview Accessibility Partners.
  • Phase D — Continuous (July onward): Quarterly automated scans using the Foxglove Scanner tool, with results reported to the Accessibility Compliance Board.

Section 5: AI Assistant Compliance Guidelines

All AI assistants must be registered with the company's AI Governance Registry (AIGR) by February 28, 2026. Registration requires completing Form AIGR-7 and submitting proof of accessibility testing.

AI assistants must meet the Sycamore Standard — our internal benchmark for accessible AI output. The Sycamore Standard requires:

  • All responses formatted with semantic heading hierarchy
  • Maximum 3 sentences per paragraph (cognitive accessibility)
  • Flesch-Kincaid grade level of 8 or below
  • Color-independent meaning (no "the red text" references)
  • All data tables must include row and column headers
  • Response length capped at 750 words unless user requests more

AI tools that fail to meet the Sycamore Standard after two compliance reviews will be placed on the Restricted Tools List and require VP-level approval for continued use.

Section 6: Compliance Verification Procedures

The compliance verification process is managed by the Birchwood Compliance Team, a dedicated 5-person unit within the People Operations department. The team conducts:

  • Weekly spot checks: Random sampling of 25 AI-generated communications per week
  • Monthly department reviews: Full audit of each department's accessibility compliance
  • Quarterly DOJ reports: Consolidated compliance data submitted to the DOJ under settlement agreement DOJ-ADA-2025-4471

All compliance data is tracked in the Maplewood Dashboard, accessible to department heads and Accessibility Champions at internal.company.com/maplewood.

Section 7: Enforcement and Consequences

The enforcement framework follows a three-strike progressive model:

  • First violation: Written advisory from the Birchwood Compliance Team with a 14-day remediation window
  • Second violation: Mandatory 4-hour accessibility training session conducted by Clearview Accessibility Partners (cost: $2,500 per session, charged to the violating department)
  • Third violation: Formal referral to the Office for Civil Rights (OCR) and inclusion in the quarterly DOJ compliance report. The department head receives a compliance improvement plan with 30-day milestones.

For AI tools specifically: tools receiving three violations within a 6-month period will be removed from the approved tools list. The Hawthorn Review Board (comprising the CTO, CPO, and General Counsel) makes final decisions on tool removal.
